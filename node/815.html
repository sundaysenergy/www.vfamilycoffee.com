Here in Minnesota we don't pay sales tax on food and so we like to joke when folks ask about it from time to time, "nope, no sales tax, coffee is essential nutrition, you know!" Joking aside, while we would never go so far as to claim that coffee is a health food, it has been nice to read of research indicating there are health benefits to this stimulating beverage. Since we hinted at writing about health last month -- that's what you'll find below -- a cursory look at coffee and your health. You'll also find the much anticipated holiday flavor list for 2012 and a number of great alternative gift buying opportunities.

To Your Health
Somehow it seems appropriate to talk about Health matters on the day after Halloween when many of us (especially our younger family members) have overdosed on sweets. Recently a coffee customer sent us a link from Dr. Oz's website, RealAge, sharing research that coffee reduces the risk of several types of cancer. It also appears to be beneficial in warding off Alzheimer's disease and Parkinson's and in fighting diabetes. We did a quick google search and found the website Coffee and Health that offers a nice summary of recent health research about coffee. It seems pretty balanced, but keep in mind the website is sponsored by 7 major European coffee companies!

Now, we have no intention of exploiting what seems to be ever changing research results for our marketing purposes, but we are curious if there is any basis to all these claims. With a little more internet research, we came across a nice summary article by Dr Hensrud at the Mayo Clinic and that seemed to be a reliable enough source. His understated conclusion, "The best answer may be that for most people the health benefits [of coffee] outweigh the risks." That's good enough for us. Feel free to forward any other news you might run across about coffee and health and we'll all continue to learn together.

Announcing our 2012 Holiday Flavors
Our latest holiday flavor survey was met with incredible enthusiasm. 144 people voted, most of them on the first day. The first 50 votes came through in the first hour!! Knowing your anticipation for this seasonal event, we won't keep you waiting any longer. Here are the winning flavors for 2012:

  • Wind Chill -- Rich cream flavor with signature notes of vanilla, caramel, and dark chocolate has all the warmth you'll need during those frosty winter nights.
  • Gingerbread Spice - Grandma's gingerbread cookies never smelled so good. Freshly baked characteristics of rich spices, vanilla, nutmeg, cinnamon and ginger that will make your mouth water.
  • Drunken Uncle -- Picture yourself around the fireplace basking in the soothing tastes of Kahlua and Irish Cream laced with Gran Marnier.
  • Almond Toffee - A crunchy butterscotch toffee with the sweetness of brown sugar and the nuttiness of toasted almonds.

All four of these flavors will be available in the coming 3-4 months in 8 oz packages, whole bean and ground and in limited quantities of decaf for $6.50. While we tried to order enough of each flavor, it is impossible to know for sure. Get your order to us quickly to make sure you get what you want. NEW THIS YEAR, you can also use our website store to place your order. (And of course our regular flavors continue to be available during this time as well.)
